\chapter*{Abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}
This thesis develops a service-level quantitative framework for
measuring technical debt in mission-critical trading systems. Using a
multi-criteria decision-making approach based on TOPSIS, the framework
evaluates services across five dimensions: code quality, infrastructure,
architecture, documentation, and operations. The model is validated
against empirical data from a Swiss securities trading environment,
demonstrating clear discriminant validity and practical utility for IT
governance.

\chapter{Introduction}\label{introduction}

\section{Background and Motivation}\label{background-and-motivation}

The increasing complexity of mission-critical trading systems has
introduced significant challenges in maintaining software quality over
time. Technical debt --- the implied cost of rework caused by choosing
expedient solutions over more robust approaches --- has become a
critical concern in financial technology environments where system
reliability is paramount.

\section{Problem Statement}\label{problem-statement}

Despite widespread acknowledgement of technical debt as a key risk
factor in enterprise IT, there remains a lack of standardised,
quantitative frameworks for measuring and managing it specifically
within mission-critical trading infrastructures. Current approaches are
largely qualitative and inconsistent, making it difficult to prioritise
remediation efforts effectively.

\section{Research Aims and
Objectives}\label{research-aims-and-objectives}

This thesis aims to develop a service-level quantitative framework for
measuring technical debt in mission-critical trading systems. The
specific objectives are:

\begin{enumerate}
\def\labelenumi{\arabic{enumi}.}
\tightlist
\item
  To review existing literature on technical debt measurement frameworks
  and service management practices
\item
  To identify and validate a set of quantifiable technical debt
  indicators relevant to trading systems
\item
  To design and apply a multi-criteria decision-making model (TOPSIS)
  for scoring and prioritising technical debt
\item
  To evaluate the framework against real-world data from a
  mission-critical trading environment
\item
  To assess the practical utility of the framework for IT managers and
  decision-makers
\end{enumerate}

\section{Research Questions}\label{research-questions}

The study is guided by the following primary research question:

\begin{quote}
\emph{How can technical debt in mission-critical trading systems be
measured quantitatively at the service level?}
\end{quote}

Secondary questions include:

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  Which indicators most reliably capture technical debt in trading
  infrastructure?
\item
  How can TOPSIS be applied to rank services by accumulated technical
  debt?
\item
  What thresholds distinguish acceptable from critical debt levels?
\end{itemize}

\section{Significance of the Study}\label{significance-of-the-study}

This research contributes to both academic knowledge and industry
practice by providing a reproducible, data-driven methodology that
bridges IT service management and software quality engineering.

\section{Thesis Structure}\label{thesis-structure}

(Ahmad \emph{et al.}, 2026)
